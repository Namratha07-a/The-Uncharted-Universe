Lore & Secrets

The history and mythology behind the games

Legendary Treasures
Uncharted 1

El Dorado

Not a city of gold but a cursed idol — a sarcophagus holding a supernatural mutagen that transforms humans into feral beasts. The Nazis found it in WWII; Nate's crew found the Nazis' bones.

⚠ Destroyed at sea
Uncharted 2

Cintamani Stone

Believed to be a giant sapphire from Shambhala, the stone is actually solidified resin from the Tree of Life — granting superhuman strength and regeneration to those who drink its sap.

⚠ Tree destroyed, stone inert
Uncharted 3

Iram of the Pillars

The "Atlantis of the Sands" — a real city buried under the Arabian desert. Its waters cause horrific hallucinations and madness. Marlowe sought to weaponize the supply and control world leaders.

⚠ City swallowed by sands
Uncharted 4

Libertalia's Treasury

The accumulated plunder of 12 pirate captains who founded a utopian colony in Madagascar. Henry Avery's hoard — the largest private fortune in the 17th century — hidden in a ghost city.

✓ Partially recovered by Nate & Elena
The Lost Legacy

Tusk of Ganesh

The broken tusk of the Hindu god Ganesh, used to write the Mahabharata. Hidden within the ruins of the Hoysala Empire in the Western Ghats of India. A national treasure and cultural symbol.

✓ Recovered & preserved
All Games

Drake's Ring & Journal

Sir Francis Drake's ring — engraved "Sic Parvis Magna" (greatness from small beginnings) — and his personal journal are the recurring key to every adventure. Nate never removes the ring.

★ Nate's most prized possession
Real History vs. Uncharted Fiction
⚓
Francis Drake
Real Person
Reality

Sir Francis Drake (c.1540–1596) was an English privateer and the second person to circumnavigate the globe. He raided Spanish ships under Queen Elizabeth I's approval and was considered a hero in England but a pirate by Spain.

Uncharted

Drake supposedly faked his own death, hid the secret of El Dorado in a coffin at sea, and left a coded journal for his descendants. Nate claims to be one of those descendants — though the games subtly imply this may be wishful thinking.

🗺️
Marco Polo's Lost Fleet
Real Mystery
Reality

Marco Polo's 1292 return voyage from China to Persia is genuinely mysterious. He departed with 600 sailors and arrived with 18. He never explained what happened to the rest. Historians have debated it for centuries.

Uncharted

In Among Thieves, Polo discovered Shambhala and was forced to destroy any evidence of its existence. The 582 who died were transformed by the Cintamani Stone's power. Polo smuggled one blue resin lamp out of the hidden city.

🏴‍☠️
Henry Avery
Real Pirate
Reality

Henry Avery (1659–c.1696) was a real English pirate who captured the Ganj-i-Sawai in 1695 — the richest pirate heist in history. He stole £600,000 (hundreds of millions today) and vanished. His fate is genuinely unknown.

Uncharted

In A Thief's End, Avery co-founded Libertalia with 11 other pirates in Madagascar. Greed eventually turned the captains against each other. Avery was the last survivor, living out his days as an old man guarding a ghost city full of treasure.

🏛️
Hoysala Empire
Real Civilization
Reality

The Hoysala Empire ruled South India from the 10th–14th centuries. Their temple architecture — featuring extraordinarily detailed carvings on every surface — is considered among humanity's greatest artistic achievements. Halebidu was their capital.

Uncharted

The Lost Legacy recreates actual Hoysala temple architecture with stunning accuracy. The Tusk of Ganesh is woven into genuine Hindu mythology. Naughty Dog consulted extensively with historians to ensure respectful representation.

Some facts
01

Naughty Dog spent over 3 years making Uncharted 4, employing a team of 300+ developers. The game's opening sequence alone was redesigned multiple times.

02

Nathan Drake was partly inspired by Harrison Ford's Indiana Jones, but his personality draws heavily from Johnny Knoxville's everyman recklessness.

03

The train sequence in Among Thieves is widely considered one of the greatest action set-pieces in gaming history. It was recreated almost shot-for-shot in fan film tributes.

04

Sic Parvis Magna — Francis Drake's real historical motto — translates to "Greatness from small beginnings" and appears engraved on Nate's ring throughout every game.

05

Nolan North, who voices Nate, was also the voice of The Penguin in Batman: Arkham City, Deadpool, and hundreds of other video game characters. He's one of the most prolific voice actors in history.

06

The Madagascar open-world segment of Uncharted 4 was Naughty Dog's first experiment with large open environments — paving the way for The Last of Us Part II's Seattle sections.

07

In A Thief's End's epilogue, Nate and Elena's daughter Cassie discovers her parents' adventuring history. The game's final collectible — Drake's journal — is found in her room.

08

The entire Uncharted franchise has won more than 200 Game of the Year awards across its five entries — more than almost any other PlayStation exclusive franchise in history.
